About PCOD

Empowering Women. Spreading Awareness. Building Healthier Futures.

Polycystic Ovarian Disease (PCOD) is a hormonal condition that affects many women of reproductive age. It happens when the ovaries release immature or partially mature eggs, which over time can turn into cysts. Women with PCOD may experience irregular or missed periods, weight gain, acne, hair thinning or excessive hair growth, mood changes, and difficulty conceiving. The exact cause is not fully known, but genetics, lifestyle, and hormonal imbalance play a major role. While PCOD is a long-term condition, it can be managed with healthy lifestyle changes, proper diet, regular exercise, stress management, and medical support. Early awareness and care not only reduce symptoms but also help prevent future complications like diabetes, infertility, and heart problems.
